What is occupational therapy?

Occupational therapy is client-centered healthcare that helps individuals perform daily, meaningful activities after injury, illness, or disability. For children, OT often focuses on skills needed for school, play, self-care, and social participation.

Common goals we support

  • Handwriting and classroom routines
  • Fine motor skills (cutting, grasp, coordination)
  • Attention, planning, and executive functioning
  • Sensory processing and regulation strategies

For families and caregivers

Caregivers are part of the team. We share home strategies, community supports, and guidance to help you support your child between sessions.
